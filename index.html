The Jaagruthi

Jaagruthi, is an event especially aimed at instilling and enthusing school students with the spirit of Materials Science and Engineering. It is a one day event to bring together creative minds of reputed schools and help them know more about Metallurgical engineering and Material Sciences. It consists of competitions, quizzes and a Lecture by one of a reputed senior faculty members of IIT Madras. This event will give the students a very good exposure to the activities happening at IIT Madras and also inspire them to join the field of Engineering. Having witnessed a participation of over 500 students in its first edition. Jaagruthi is back with its second edition during Amalgam. This time bigger and better! The concept of “How things work” a basic level quiz is conducted in every school, after which students are selected to come and attend the big event here at IIT Madras on 22nd August, 2014. Having reached out to approximately 2200 participants in around 22 schools in Chennai. We are all set for the upcoming version of Jaagruthi.

SOME

The most awaited event of this material science weekend ..so called Spirit Of Materials Engineering aka SoME brings you exhibition of experiments that unites radically different manifestations and improvisations on varied themes of undercooling of water, meissner effect, piezoelectric material and many more... come and learn from this exhibition which is a open collaboration from professors and students, with a specific view to collaborating on scientific projects. Increase the aura of your scientific imaginations, infact get a chance of hands on on certain experiments and exhibits. At the end of the visit, engage yourself with a quick quiz and win goodies along with the take away knowledge. Feel free to drop in any ideas that you have on material science.

reaching IITm

1. Local train: If you do not have heavy luggage, the MRTS Station called "Park Town" on the Beach-Velachery line, opposite to Chennai-Central station, is a good option. From there, you can take a train to "Kasturba Nagar (Adyar)" (which comes after the kotturpuram station) and reach for Madhya Kailash temple and then walk towards west on Sardar Patel Road to campus main gate. From the gate there are IITM buses to the hostels, every 20 minutes. 2. You can catch a bus just outside the Chennai Central. 18B and 5C are the direct buses to IITM (Get down at Gandhi Mandapam/IITM). From the gate there are IITM buses to the hostels, every 20 minutes. 3. Hire an auto/taxi to IIT Madras. You can catch institute buses from the IIT Madras main gate to control room. The fare will be roughly Rs.350-380 for a Taxi and Rs.200-250 for an Auto.

Casting

People wanting a Hands-on experience on metal casting, hop on board for the second edition of Metal Casting Workshop here at Amalgam. Thermo CALC

Amalgam would like to take the privilege to introduce you to SECOND edition of our Computational workshop on the 23rd of August 2014. From calculating amounts of phases, their compositions, to thermochemical data calculations like enthalpies, heat capacity and activities we would like you to experience the variety “Thermo Calc” has for its users. A software well suited for major thermodynamically computations, calculation of thermodynamical data required for phase diagram formulation to formulation of phase diagram itself. We ensure you that under the guidance of one among the most knowledgeable persons in the field of Phase Diagrams, Professor K C Hari Kumar, you will have an exciting and a high learning experience in the vast field of computational modelling.
